Challenges

After Paul's talk, Felipe told the residents that we had to fork a branch of his Tic Tac Toe that was working only for a 3x3 board, and try to make it work for a 4x4 board in just one hour. If we couldn't make it, we should make a pull request anyway, with whatever we had managed to accomplish. He told us that the important part was the pull request itself, not so much the code.

I paired up with Otto, who hadn't done anything with Java before, and we were scared that in an hour we wouldn't be able to make it work for both a 3x3 and a 4x4 board, so we decided to overwrite what Felipe had for a 3x3 board with the code for a 4x4 board. The cell numbers were hard-coded because that's the easiest solution for a 3x3 board, but we should have changed that to make it work for more general cases, instead of hard-coding the numbers as we did.

Since Otto is a student who hasn't done much with code before, and I'm a resident who has been programming for so long, I feel it was my responsibility to act cool under pressure and do the right thing. Instead, I freaked out and delivered something that was working only for one case, when I was given something that was working for all cases. I'm leaving this here to make myself ashamed of me in the future, when I hope I will be a better me and won't do this ugly things. This was a bit of another "earth open up and swallow me in rite nao" situation.

Felipe sat with us and asked us if we would deliver that to a client (the answer was no), and why didn't we do it the right way (the answer was, we couldn't handle the pressure). So, this is a challenge I have to overcome: to keep cool in times of tight deadlines.

Things I want to change

There are many times in my life when I feel a bit like a caveman. Now that I'm in this city where everybody is so elegant, nice and smart, it shows up much more. These are some things I would like to work on in the near future:

  • Stop being a caveman and learn some manners. I'm too spontaneous, this is going to kill me in the future.
  • Because I'm a caveman, I fear that sometimes I could have said or done something offensive or just rude. I have to pay more attention and watch out.
  • Work on my Spanglish, and try to become as articulate as Jarkyn, as well as learn how to communicate more accurately in general.
  • Stop always trying to say something smart. I'm not in that world any-more. And I'm not smart, so it takes a huge time to find something smart to say. On top of that, I have to make it sound smart in English, at which I fail miserably. The result of this is that I block, and stay staring at the person in front of me, who is waiting for me to say something, but after all that waiting, I only give them a nervous laugh or make a bad joke. People get bored and don't give second chances! If I don't have anything smart to say, I think it's better to just not say anything.
  • Talking about jokes, I have to stop being the "clown wannabe" and be more serious. Unless I can think of a very good joke, I should really remember to shut up.
  • Despite my efforts of acting as a strong person, I always come out as "cute", because I'm too candid. It's about time to wise up.

Would these suffice to turn me into a decent person? I'm sure not... but they're a start.
