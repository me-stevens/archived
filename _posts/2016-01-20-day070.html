Nice things

I was a bit sad that my codebar student didn't come today. I had all sorts of things prepared for the site I wanted to build for her. But I think the location was not good for her to reach it on time after work. I hope she can come next Wednesday.

Anyway, Sophie came to codebar and I could finally pair with her doing the bowling kata. Long ago, when I met her at the uncodebar conference, she told me she wanted to learnt some TDD and I suggested she could do that kata. Not because there was anything special with it, but because it came with a Power Point presentation that explained step by step how to go through the process. I found it very handy when you are starting to figure out how to TDD. And... she followed my advice! She came to codebar to do that kata.

She doesn't know Java though, so we did it in Ruby. I think the steps are the same no matter the language, and the learnings too. So we went through it and, even though I have looked at the syntax of Ruby at some point, and tried to sit through tutorials to learn the language, my brain is a bit fucked up by C-like languages, which is what I 've used my whole life, so I'm not very good at Ruby. We had some compiler problems, and I learnt that the Ruby compiler error messages don't help you much. Hopefully, Rabea and Felipe were near and they helped us fix the problem.

I also had some variable initialized at the beginning of a class, and Felipe told me "That's so Java", lol. Then he told me that once I learn functional languages, I won't even have to declare any variables. I hope I can reach that stage soon!

After that, we went to the pub and even Denise joined us for a while to share some funny language swear words. With Germany, Uruguay, France, Spain and China represented in our tiny group, laughs were assured.
