Anyway, life is good, the future is bright, and thanks to Skim, I learnt about the difference between recursion and iteration.

Yesterday, in my IPM, Jim advised me not to use a recursive method for the run() method of my EchoServer class, but use a loop instead. I was curious about the differences in performance between a recursive method that goes on forever or an infinite loop.

Recursion creates a new stack every time the function is called, and that could cause a stack overflow if my user kept typing things forever that were not "exit". A loop keeps everything in one stack, so you could never have a stack overflow (you could have other problems though, if you had an infinite loop).
